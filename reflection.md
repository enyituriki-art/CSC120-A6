What are your initial impressions of how `Unit Testing` affects your programming?
What worked, what didn't, what advice would you give someone taking this course in the future?


Before unit testing, I used to test my code by writing one big main method and printing things out just to see if they worked, but this assignment made me think about each method on its own instead of putting everything together. When I started testing each method separately, the bugs were much easier to spot and it felt more organized than writing one big main method. What worked best for me was keeping the tests small and focused because that made it really clear where the logic went wrong. An advice I would give to future students is to write tests early instead of waiting until the end and to read the method descriptions carefully because most bugs come from the code not matching the intended behavior. Overall I enjoyed how unit testing made my programming feel more organized and less like guessing and it helped me understand my code in a much clearer way.
